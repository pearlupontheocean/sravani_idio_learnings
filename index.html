Flow

The concept of Flow, as defined by Mihaly Csikszentmihalyi, offers profound insights into the psychology of optimal performance. It encapsulates the experience of being fully immersed in an activity, resulting in heightened focus and an altered sense of time.

A video of musician Mohini Dey was shown in class to illustrate this. She became one with her instrument, a perfect example of flow characterized by:

  • Focus
  • Timelessness
  • The state of being one with the act

As a singer, I resonated deeply with this concept, recognizing the state intuitively through my performances.

Attitude of Perfection & Excellence

An attitude of perfection fosters excellence, attention to detail, and a commitment to delivering the best results. It drives continuous improvement, builds credibility, and inspires others, ensuring high-quality outcomes in both personal and professional endeavors.

Synergy & Harmony

Synergy magnifies collective efforts, creating outcomes greater than individual contributions, while harmony ensures seamless coexistence and mutual respect. Together, they foster an environment of unity and productivity.

Fulfillment in Life and Work

Fulfillment and purpose give life meaning, guiding actions and decisions toward personal growth and contribution. They inspire resilience, helping individuals lead more content, impactful, and satisfying lives.

Myers-Briggs Type Indicator (MBTI)

The MBTI evaluates personality based on four dichotomies:

  • Introversion (I) vs. Extraversion (E)
  • Sensing (S) vs. Intuition (N)
  • Thinking (T) vs. Feeling (F)
  • Judging (J) vs. Perceiving (P)

After taking the test, I discovered my personality type is INTJ. The test provided valuable insights into my traits and tendencies.

Kintsugi and Vulnerability

Kintsugi teaches us to celebrate imperfections. Vulnerability, often mistaken for weakness, is a strength that fosters empathy and trust. Expressing vulnerability requires courage and authenticity, creating an environment of compassion both personally and professionally.

Intrinsic Motivation

Motivation plays a pivotal role in achieving self-actualization, as introduced by Abraham Maslow in his Hierarchy of Needs. The Cognitive Evaluation Theory further distinguishes between intrinsic and extrinsic motivators, emphasizing the importance of internal drivers like achievement and competence.
